# Case Study: Mid-Market Tech Operations — Control-Dependency Archetype
*Anonymized | McDonald, E.M. — March 2026*

---

## Profile Summary

**Environment:** Mid-size technology company, operations/manufacturing management
**Tenure:** 15-25 years in technical operations
**Training exposure:** Company-sponsored leadership programs, compliance certifications
**Kenney 2x2 Classification:** Q2 — Sophisticated Tribalism

---

## Behavioral Fingerprint

The tech operations control-dependency profile differs from retail in vocabulary but not in structure. Key behaviors:

- Process ownership defended as institutional knowledge gatekeeping
- Data and analytics tools introduced by team members treated as authority challenges
- Approval bottlenecks centralized in the manager rather than delegated
- Team output measured by activity and hours rather than outcomes

---

## The Structural Setup

- Technical compliance metrics (defect rates, audit performance, SLA adherence) dominate the KPI architecture
- Long-term costs of control management (talent attrition, innovation suppression) are not tracked
- Leadership training is compliance-driven — HR needs the box checked
- Promotion history in the chain reflects same control-dependency profile

---

## The Data Tool Resistance Pattern

When team members introduce analytics tools, dashboards, or data-driven process improvements, the Q2 operations manager experiences this as a structural threat rather than a capability addition. The auxiliary hypothesis activates: "Our environment is too complex / too specific / too regulated for that approach to work here."

The tools that would make the team's performance visible independently of manager proximity are the tools most likely to be blocked, deprioritized, or allowed to stall.

---

## EBT Pathway Diagnosis

**Pathway 1 (Training Data Contamination):** Technical expertise was the pathway to management. The behavioral model inherited was control-through-expertise, not leadership-through-enablement.

**Pathway 2 (SCE):** Promotion criteria in technical operations reward technical authority and compliance output. High-autonomy team outcomes are not captured in the evaluation system.

**Pathway 3 (OL):** Leadership program completions documented. Behavioral change measurement absent. The certification launders the control-dependency style into a credentialed leadership profile.
