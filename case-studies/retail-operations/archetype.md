## Behavioral Fingerprint

This profile represents the canonical retail GM who has been in-role long enough that the environment and the identity have merged. Key observable behaviors:

- Scheduling structured to maximize personal floor presence
- Resistance to data-driven decision tools that would shift authority to analytics rather than intuition
- Framing of high-performer departures as individual choices rather than systemic signals
- Post-training return to baseline within 2-4 weeks — vocabulary absorbed, behavior unchanged

## The Structural Setup

This manager operates in an environment that was structurally designed to produce and sustain this profile:

- Weekly shrinkage, comp, and labor KPIs reward compliance production
- Annual leadership training produces certifications without behavioral accountability
- District-level management promoted from the same control-dependency cohort
- High turnover (60%+ annually) continuously confirms the surveillance assumption

## The Self-Sealing Loop

When high performers leave: attributed to the individual, not the management culture.
When performance dips during absence: attributed to the need for more presence.
When data tools are introduced: framed as a threat to institutional knowledge rather than a complement to it.

Every outcome that challenges the model gets routed through the auxiliary hypothesis. Every outcome that confirms it gets filed as evidence.
