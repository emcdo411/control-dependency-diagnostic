## Primary Meta-Analyses

### Burke & Day (1986)
**Citation:** Burke, M.J., & Day, R.R. (1986). A cumulative study of the effectiveness of managerial training. *Journal of Applied Psychology, 71*(2), 232-245.

**Key finding:** Leadership training reliably produces knowledge transfer. Behavioral change and organizational performance impact were not demonstrated at scale.

**Framework use:** Establishes the knowledge/behavior gap as empirically documented, not anecdotal.

---

### Collins & Holton (2004)
**Citation:** Collins, D.B., & Holton, E.F. (2004). The effectiveness of managerial leadership development programs: A meta-analysis of studies from 1982 to 2001. *Human Resource Development Quarterly, 15*(2), 217-248.

**Key finding:**
- Knowledge gains: reliable
- Attitude/motivation shifts: moderate, temporary
- Behavioral change: weak, non-durable, regresses to baseline within 90 days without structural reinforcement
- Organizational performance impact: near-zero when controlling for other variables

**Framework use:** Primary empirical anchor for the Objectivity Laundering diagnosis.

---

### Avolio et al. (2009)
**Citation:** Avolio, B.J., Reichard, R.J., Hannah, S.T., Walumbwa, F.O., & Chan, A. (2009). A meta-analytic review of leadership impact research. *The Leadership Quarterly, 20*(5), 764-784.

**Key finding:** Replicates Collins & Holton findings on behavioral change durability.

## Organizational Behavior Foundations

### Edmondson (1999)
**Citation:** Edmondson, A. (1999). Psychological safety and learning behavior in work teams. *Administrative Science Quarterly, 44*(2), 350-383.

**Key finding:** Teams with high psychological safety outperform surveilled teams on innovation, error correction, and discretionary effort.

**Framework use:** Empirical counter to the "cats away, mice play" belief.

---

### Heilman (Lack of Fit Model)
**Citation:** Heilman, M.E. (1983). Sex bias in work settings: The lack of fit model. *Research in Organizational Behavior, 5*, 269-298.

**Key finding:** Female managers in agentic roles face prescriptive norm backlash for the same behaviors that male managers are rewarded for.

**Framework use:** Gender dynamics layer — explains structural pressures that produce and reinforce control-dependency in female operations managers.

---

### Westwood et al. (2025)
**Citation:** Westwood, S., et al. (2025). The limits of interventions to reduce affective polarization. *Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences.*

**Key finding:** Individual-level interventions produce small, non-durable effects. Structural forces continuously overwhelm individual psychology.

**Framework use:** Structural overdetermination threshold — Westwood Effect Size Realism applied to leadership development interventions.

---

### Toegel — The Confidence Myth (2025)
**Citation:** Toegel, G. (2025). *The Confidence Myth.* IMD Business School.

**Key finding:** Confidence gaps in female managers are structural perception problems, not dispositional deficits.

**Framework use:** Toegel Confidence Architecture layer — explains the "if I'm not here, authority will be challenged" structural undercurrent.
