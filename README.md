## What This Is

This repository contains an integrated diagnostic framework built using AI to identify, name, and structurally explain why control-dependency management styles persist in organizations — despite decades of leadership development investment — and why the industry that is supposed to fix them is structurally incentivized not to.

This is not a critique of individual managers. It is a structural systems diagnosis.

---

## The Core Finding

The  leadership development industry produces:

| What Is Sold | What the Research Shows |
|---|---|
| Behavioral transformation | Knowledge transfer only |
| Leadership capability development | Temporary attitude shifts |
| Organizational performance improvement | Near-zero performance impact |
| Culture change | Compliance documentation |

**Empirical anchors:** Burke & Day (1986); Collins & Holton (2004); Avolio et al. (2009)

## Market Gap

What is **not** being built anywhere in the commercial AI coaching landscape:

1. A diagnostic that identifies a manager's **foundational mindset architecture** before designing any intervention
2. An audit of whether the **organizational KPI structure** will reinforce or neutralize behavioral change
3. **90/180-day behavioral outcome measurement** against baseline with consequence-linked accountability
4. A system that explicitly names when an organization's **promotion criteria are selecting for the very style** the intervention is supposed to change

That fourth point is why it has not been built commercially. This repository is the foundation for building it.
